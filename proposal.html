Find Food Seattle

Philanthropist Esther Ndichu, in her TED Talk, said that, "hunger isn't a food issue, it's a logistics issue." Across Washington, 8.3 percent of households were experiencing food insecurity. This statistic notably doesn't account for the homeless population of Seattle, which includes tens of thousands of people who struggle with finding meals.

There are many organizations that have been built to assist with the logistical troubles of providing food to unhoused people, but despite this the issue still persists. Although many drives are available to offer assistance and accept donations, the relationship between donators, food banks, and those who use food banks has yet to be centralized within one platform. There is also no consistent platform for promoting food drive events, which may dissuade people looking to donate if they cannot find a receiver near them.

ShelterApp is an app dedicated to providing immediate access and knowledge of social services to its users. Although this app is useful, and has a feature where users can look up food banks near them, the App lacks a website with the same function. By creating a platform based on the web, users who don't have access to an iOS or Android device could still find this service through avenues like public library computers or devices purchased through government programs like ConnectAll. This problem space involves establishments accepting food donations and those who use those establishments, as well as potential donators.

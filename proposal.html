Find Food Seattle

Problem/Data Domain

Philanthropist Esther Ndichu, in her TED Talk, said that, "hunger isn't a food issue, it's a logistics issue." Across Washington, 8.3 percent of households were experiencing food insecurity. This statistic notably doesn't account for the homeless population of Seattle, which includes tens of thousands of people who struggle with finding meals.

There are many organizations that have been built to assist with the logistical troubles of providing food to unhoused people, but despite this the issue still persists. Although many drives are available to offer assistance and accept donations, the relationship between donators, food banks, and those who use food banks has yet to be centralized within one platform. There is also no consistent platform for promoting food drive events, which may dissuade people looking to donate if they cannot find a receiver near them.

ShelterApp is an app dedicated to providing immediate access and knowledge of social services to its users. Although this app is useful, and has a feature where users can look up food banks near them, the App lacks a website with the same function. By creating a platform based on the web, users who don't have access to an iOS or Android device could still find this service through avenues like public library computers or devices purchased through government programs like ConnectAll. This problem space involves establishments accepting food donations and those who use those establishments, as well as potential donators.

App Description

The users of this application will include Seattle-based food donors (restaurants, grocery stores, and individuals) with excess food who want to donate to prevent waste and support others in their community; food banks, organizations, or individuals seeking food donations; and other volunteers who may offer transportation help for the food. This app would have a significant impact on minority communities, especially low-income communities, by improving access to food resources, and thus, reducing food insecurity in the Seattle area.

The first significant feature of this web app will allow users (food donors) to post and input details about the excess food they may have, including type, quantity, pictures, expiration dates, and location/delivery preferences. This way of posting has the potential to introduce new potential donors who work at private businesses to cast a wide net when looking for places to donate their excess food. The second significant feature of this web app will be a chat feature that allows users looking to pick up donated food to coordinate with the initial poster, allowing for a direct line of communication between donors and recipients to clarify details and coordinate food pickup. This will allow for food arrangements to be made quickly and accurately. The additional smaller feature will be a search and filter function that enables users to find available donation postings that meet their specific needs, such as food type, proximity, and expiration date, making it easier to source out food. The type of food sold by a given distributor can be filtered, allowing easier access for those looking for food donations with an allergy or other dietary restriction.

On our platform, users will encounter information in the form of listings of available food donations, including descriptions, quantities, and locations; and chat messages between donors and recipients. The app aims to mitigate food waste and insecurity by establishing a direct link between those with surplus food and those in need. It promotes a community-driven approach, nurturing a culture of mutual aid in the Seattle area, and streamlining the process of food distribution.
